D-One Soccer League

D-One Soccer League By-Laws

Adopted on: 4/25/2025

Article I: Purpose

The purpose of D-One Soccer League is to promote fair play, sportsmanship, and community engagement through organized soccer competition for young adults aged 18 to 40.

The D-One Soccer League is an affiliate Member of Mass Soccer. Competitions sanctioned by the D-One Soccer League shall be considered sanctioned by Mass Soccer.

Article II: Membership & Eligibility

1. Eligibility

Participation is open to individuals aged 18–40 who are properly registered and in good standing with Mass Soccer. All players must sign a waiver before participating in league activities.

2. Registration

Players and teams must register and submit all required fees prior to the season’s start in order to participate.

3. Code of Conduct

Unsportsmanlike conduct, including but not limited to fighting, harassment, or abusive language, may result in suspension or expulsion from the league.

Article III: League Structure

1. Divisions

Teams will be divided into two groups: Group A and Group B for tournament play.

2. Season Timeline

The league season runs from mid-June to mid-September, concluding with a playoff tournament.

3. Playoff Format

The top four teams from each group will advance to the playoffs. The #1 overall seed will face the #8 overall seed, based on a combined ranking system.

Article IV: Rules of Play

1. Rules

Matches will follow FIFA Laws of the Game with the following modifications:

  • Unlimited substitutions (In and Out) are allowed during the regular season.
  • Unlimited substitutions but no re-entry are allowed during playoff matches.

2. Officials

Games will be officiated by USSF certified referees or approved volunteer officials.

Article V: Governance

1. League Management

The league will be governed by four members: President, Vice President and Commissioner, Gameday Manager, and Marketing/Entertainment Director, who are chosen by a majority vote of the team representatives. Additional positions may be appointed as needed.

2. Decision-Making

Major decisions (e.g., rule changes, fee adjustments) require a majority vote of the League Board or approval from a majority of team representatives.

Article VI: Financial Policies

1. Fees

League fees will cover referee compensation, field rental, and equipment. Refunds will only be issued if the season is canceled.

2. Sponsorships

Teams may obtain sponsorships. All sponsor logos must be approved by the League Board before use on uniforms or promotional materials.

Article VII: Discipline & Appeals

1. Violations

A red card results in an automatic one-game suspension. Repeat offenses may lead to further disciplinary action, including expulsion.

2. Appeals Process

Players or teams may appeal disciplinary actions in writing within 48 hours of notification. Appeals will be reviewed by the League Board.

Article VIII: Amendments

By-laws may be amended by a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the League Board or a majority vote of team representatives.

Article IX: Additional Policies

1. Insurance Requirement

All players must have personal health insurance or be covered under a league-provided policy.

2. Weather Policy

In the event of inclement weather, games may be postponed or canceled. The League Board will notify teams of rescheduling details.

3. Social Media Guidelines

All participants must conduct themselves respectfully on social media. Offensive or inflammatory posts related to the league may result in disciplinary action.
